Well-child & vaccines

Growing up is the appointment we plan for most.

Well-child visits are how we get to know your child before anyone is unwell — tracking growth, checking milestones and keeping the vaccine schedule on time. Here's roughly how the first years look.

Every child grows on their own curve. This is a general guide — your pediatrician tailors the plan to your family. Book a consultation for advice specific to you.

Newborn · first days

The first weigh-in

Feeding, weight, jaundice and sleep-safety. A calm first visit for a very new person — and for parents finding their feet.

6 weeks

First smiles, first vaccines

A growth check plus the first scheduled immunisations, given gently with plenty of cuddles afterwards.

3–6 months

Rolling, reaching, weaning soon

We track development and talk through starting solids when the time is right — no pressure, just a plan.

9–12 months

The busy year

Crawling to cruising to first steps. Growth, iron, sleep and a check that milestones are on track.

15 months – 2 years

Words and big feelings

Speech, behaviour and boosters. Toddler tantrums are normal — we'll help you tell those apart from concerns.

3–5 years · school-ready

Ready for the big room

Vision, hearing, growth and pre-school vaccines, so your child starts school steady and up to date.

6+ years & teens

Still your child's doctor

Yearly reviews, growth, mood and adolescent care — a familiar face who has known them all along.

Sick visits

When they're just not themselves.

Coughs that keep the whole house awake, a fever that won't settle, a rash that appeared overnight. Bring them in and we'll take a proper look, explain what's going on in plain language, and tell you exactly what to watch for at home.

  • Fevers, coughs, colds and sore throats
  • Tummy upsets, feeding and hydration worries
  • Rashes, ear pain and the mystery grizzles
  • Minor injuries and "should we be worried?" checks
Not sure if it can wait? Message us and we'll help you decide whether it's a clinic visit today, a call, or something to keep an eye on at home.
