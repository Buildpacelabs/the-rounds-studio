How to read your report

A number means little on its own. We show it against its range.

Every Truepath result sits on a bar between low and high, with the normal band marked in teal. One glance tells you where you stand.

Why the numbers hold

Trust isn't a promise on a wall. It's the process behind each result.

Calibrated analysers, checked daily

Instruments are run against control samples every morning before a single patient sample is processed.

Barcoded, tracked samples

Every vial is barcoded at collection, so a result can't be swapped or mixed up on its way through the lab.

Critical results, re-verified

Flagged and out-of-range criticals are re-checked before release — and the referring doctor is notified.

Reviewed by senior consultants

Pathology reports are reviewed by experienced consultant pathologists, not released by machine alone.

Before you book

The questions we're asked most.

Do I need to fast before my test?

It depends on the test. Sugar and lipid panels usually need 8–12 hours of fasting, while many others don't. Plain water is fine throughout. When you book, we'll tell you exactly what your tests need.

How soon will I get my report?

Most routine reports are ready within 24 hours, and several are same-day. Specialised tests can take longer — we'll give you a timeline when you book. Reports arrive digitally first, on WhatsApp and email.

Which areas do you cover for home collection?

We collect across Ahmedabad, most days from early morning to late evening. Share your pincode when you book and we'll confirm the nearest available slot.

Can I book without a doctor's prescription?

Many of our health packages can be booked directly. Some specialised tests need a doctor's request — if you're unsure, ask us and we'll guide you. It's always worth booking a consultation for advice specific to you.

Are my results kept confidential?

Yes. Your report is shared only with you and any doctor you nominate. We don't sell or share your health data.

Will someone explain what my report means?

Every report shows reference ranges beside each value, with clear flags where something is out of range. For advice on what your specific results mean for you, book a consultation with your doctor.
