Dialysis, made calmer.

Most of our patients spend a few hours with us, a few times a week. We design every part of that time — the chair, the pace, the people — so it feels less like a procedure and more like a routine you can live around.

Trained dialysis technicians

Care plans in plain language

Kidney care & transplant support

Looking after your kidneys for the long run.

Whether you are newly diagnosed or living with chronic kidney disease, our nephrologists help you understand what is happening and what you can do — one clear, unhurried step at a time.

  • CKD management — regular reviews, medication guidance and monitoring to help slow progression.
  • Blood pressure & diabetes-related kidney care — co-ordinated with your other doctors.
  • Diet & lifestyle guidance — practical advice from a renal dietitian, built around how you actually eat.
  • Transplant support — evaluation, referral and steady follow-up before and after a kidney transplant.

Every plan is personal — book a consultation for advice specific to you.

Good to know

Questions patients often ask.

What is a dialysis session here actually like?

You settle into a reclining chair in a quiet bay, connected to the dialysis machine by a trained technician. Most sessions last a few hours. You are welcome to rest, read, watch something or simply chat with the team, and a family member can sit with you.

Do you help with home dialysis?

Yes. For patients it suits, our nephrologists discuss home dialysis options, help with training and set up a follow-up routine so you feel confident and supported. Whether it is right for you is something we work out together in consultation.

Can my family stay with me?

Absolutely. We know treatment is easier with someone familiar nearby, so there is space for a family member during dialysis and we encourage them to be part of your care conversations.

How do I start if I have just been diagnosed?

Book a consultation and bring any recent reports you have. A nephrologist will talk you through what they mean and agree the next steps with you. There is no pressure — the first visit is mostly about understanding your situation clearly.
